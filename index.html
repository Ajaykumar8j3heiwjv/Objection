The four phases of the call

01

Identify Phase

Listen past the words to the real objection. "Too expensive" might actually mean "I don't trust the outcome yet." Name the underlying concern before you respond to the surface one.

02

Connect Phase

Acknowledge the objection without arguing it. Repeat it back so the prospect feels heard — "I get why that's on your mind" — before you move to a single word of response.

03

Explore Phase

Ask a question that surfaces what's actually driving the hesitation — timing, trust, money, or permission from someone else. Don't answer a guessed objection; answer the real one.

04

Advise Phase

Respond with evidence, not reassurance — a number, a policy, an alumnus story. Close every answer with a question that moves the call forward instead of letting it go quiet.
